We Live For Passion

Bharatanatyam, an esteemed Indian classical dance form, is believed to be India's oldest classical dance tradition and is considered the progenitor of numerous other classical dance forms. Its theoretical foundation can be traced back to the 'Natya Shastra,' an ancient Sanskrit Hindu text on performing arts

The 'Abhinaya Darpana' by Nandikesvara serves as a primary textual source for studying the technique and grammar of body movements in Bharatanatyam dance.

Diploma Program

- Bharatanatyam at kRtyaVed welcomes individuals aged 8 and above who have an interest in joining.

- kRtyaVed provides certification programs in the field of Bharatanatyam.

- This comprehensive program spans seven years and imparts training in the Tanjavur style of Bharatanatyam. Students receive instruction in adaivius and theory following the established style and procedures of Shree Thanjavur Nrityashala.

Dress code

- Students are required to adhere to the dress code by wearing their uniform during each Bharatanatyam class.

Class Structure

- The academic year for each level will commence on the 1st of June and conclude on the 31st of May of the following year. Examinations are scheduled to be held during the final week of April, with summer vacations allocated to students from the 1st of May through to the 31st of May.

Payment details

All tuition fees are required to be paid in advance. In the event that a student enrolls in the middle of the academic year, they are obligated to pay for the entire annual dance program. Examination fees will be assessed separately at the time a student chooses to participate in an examination. Eligibility to take an examination is contingent upon the full payment of tuition fees for the respective academic year in which the examinations are to be undertaken.

Refunds and credits

Please note that classes are non-refundable, and there will be no reimbursements or deductions for missed sessions. We do offer adjustment classes within the same month to accommodate missed sessions. However, it's important to clarify that credits will not be issued for absences due to illness, inclement weather, or personal commitments. You are welcome to attend any of the scheduled adjustments.
